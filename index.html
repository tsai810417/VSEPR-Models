VSEPR Model

VSEPR theory is used to predict a chemical molecule geometry based on the number of lone electron pairs surrounded and number of ligands bounded to the central atom. In this application, you will illustrate the 3D views of each geometries.

Please select the generic formula below to view the sample geometry and more informations.

Note: The generic formula is represented by 'AXE method', where A represents the central atom, X represents the number of ligands bounded to central atom, and E represents the number of lone pairs of electrons surrounding the central atom.
